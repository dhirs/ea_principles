Hi [First name],

There's a comfortable story we tell ourselves about enterprise AI: build a brilliant agent, prove it works, and scale from there.

I want to challenge that, because I keep watching it fail.

A model can sail through development, demo beautifully, and run reliably in a single project — and still land the organisation on the wrong side of a regulator, a board question, or an outage. Not because the team was careless, but because **excellence at the project level was never the same thing as readiness at the enterprise level.**

The two are different disciplines. One is about building the perfect agent. The other is about what happens when you have thirty of them, built by thirty teams, all live at once.

Most organisations think Stage 2 is the finish line — every project engineered to run reliably in production. It feels like winning. It isn't. Because the moment you zoom out from one project to the whole portfolio, four things break that no individual team can fix:

1. **You can't audit what isn't consistent.** Thirty differently-built projects give a regulator thirty different answers — or none.
2. **The risk lands on a sponsor who never saw the decisions.** Accountability sits with leadership; the design calls sit with developers. Those have to be reconnected.
3. **Best practice you can't enforce is just a suggestion.** Under deadline pressure, the eval gets skipped. Only gates — not guidance — hold the floor across every team.
4. **Inconsistency becomes operations' nightmare at go-live.** Thirty bespoke agents means thirty ways to debug, patch, and recover. Unsupportable.

This is the heart of it: **individual projects optimise for launch. The enterprise has to survive auditing, accountability, enforcement, and maintenance across all of them at once.** That is not a better-model problem or a better-team problem. It's an architecture problem — shared standards, enterprise governance, central scaffolding, and controls that apply to every project rather than the lucky ones.

That shift — from building the perfect agent to building the system that makes every agent production-ready by default — is what I'm focused on, and what I'll be unpacking in [workshop / session name].

If your organisation is somewhere on that curve and wondering how to move right, I'd love for you to join.
