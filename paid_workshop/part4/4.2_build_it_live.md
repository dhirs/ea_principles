# Part 4.2 — Build It Live: The Anatomy of a Standard

**Maven one-liner:** Fully interactive — we walk every field of the chosen standard together, crowdsourcing answers and spending real time on the gate and evidence, and leave with one concrete, gated, auditable RI.

## The point of this subpart

This is the centrepiece of the workshop. Having selected one standard (4.1), the room now **builds it together, field by field.** The facilitator walks each field of the standard's anatomy and the cohort suggests answers — so they don't just see a finished standard, they *produce* one. The output is a concrete reference implementation they authored collectively.

## How it runs

Instructor-led, fully interactive. Take the sample taxonomy for the chosen standard and fill in **every field**, soliciting answers from the room at each step:

| Field | What the room decides |
|---|---|
| **Statement** | The one-sentence "what must be true" |
| **Problem** | The production failure this prevents |
| **Solution** | The control, in concrete terms |
| **Maturity Level** | Where it sits today (and target) |
| **Applicability** | `applies_to: {ML, LLM, agent}` — who it binds |
| **Criticality** | Risk/value rating |
| **Ownership** | Named owner accountable for it |
| **Enforcement Gates** | The pass/fail test — *the hard part* |
| **Change Control** | How it's versioned and revised |
| **Framework Mappings** | Anchor to the AWS GenAI Lens; `na` if it extends beyond |
| **ARB Evidence** | What gets submitted at the ARB |
| **Reference Implementation** | The concrete build it all points to |

## Spend the time on the gate and evidence

Most fields are quick. **Two are not, and they're where the workshop's whole thesis lands:**

- **Enforcement Gate** — push the room to define an *objective* pass/fail test, not a vague intention. If they can't state a check you could actually run, the standard isn't real. This is "no gate, no standard — just a wish" earned live.
- **ARB Evidence** — what *proves* the gate passed. A gate with no evidence trail can't be audited.

Spend the bulk of 4.2 here. The other ten fields can move fast; these two are the lesson.

## What they walk away with

A complete, concrete reference implementation for one standard, with ownership assigned:

- **Owned by** the Central AI Factory
- **Built by** the AI Engineering Platform Team
- **Maintained by** the Central AI Product Team
- ✓ Automatically enforced at CI/CD gates ✓ Fully auditable

## Handoff to Part 5

You've just built one standard *by hand* — slowly, deliberately, every field argued out. That sets up Part 5 perfectly: Aegis is the tool that does this at scale, so you're not filling twelve fields by hand for every standard in the catalogue.

## Takeaway for this subpart — and the close of Part 4

> You walked in with a written standard and walked out with a built, gated, auditable one — authored field by field by the whole room. That is the Monday-morning capability: not knowing *about* standards, but being able to produce one with a real gate and a real evidence trail.
