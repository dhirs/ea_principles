# Part 3.1 — The Prioritization Gate: Cheap-Many to Expensive-Few

**Maven one-liner:** You can't build every standard at once — prioritization is the gate that turns an endless backlog of blueprints into a funded, shippable few each quarter.

## The point of this subpart

Part 2 produced *written standards*. But a written standard is not yet something a team can pick up and use — it has to become a **concrete reference implementation (RI)**: artefacts, paths, CI gates, audit-ready. And you can't build them all. This subpart is about the economics that force a choice, and the gate that makes it.

## Cheap and many vs. expensive and few

Two different cost profiles sit either side of the gate:

- **RI Blueprint — cheap, many.** A sized sketch of a standard: enough to estimate effort, not built. Blueprints accumulate continuously in the catalogue as BAU. They're cheap, so you have lots of them.
- **Concrete RI — expensive, few.** The full implementation — validator, threshold table, CI gate, evidence. Expensive, so you ship only a few per cycle.

The catalogue **runs continuously**; the builds **run as quarterly sprints.** Between the two sits the gate.

## Prioritization is the gate

You can't fund every blueprint, so prioritization **ranks the sized blueprints and sets the fixed quarterly agenda.** Rank candidates by the dimensions that actually matter:

- **Criticality** — how much risk/value rides on it.
- **Difficulty** — how hard the RI is to build.
- **Reusability** — how many projects it serves.
- **Enforcement cost** — what it costs to gate and operate.

The output is each quarter's build list — and **each prioritised blueprint becomes one funded mini-project**: its own budget, a named owner, a RACI chart, built out into a full reference implementation.

## Takeaway for this subpart

> Blueprints are cheap and many; concrete RIs are expensive and few. Prioritization is the gate between them — it ranks the backlog by risk, reuse, difficulty, and enforcement cost, and turns the quarter's winners into funded, owned builds.
