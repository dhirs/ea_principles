Most AI projects don't fail in the lab. They fail in production.

And the ones that survive production? They still fail the enterprise.

I've been mapping AI delivery into three stages (diagram below 👇):

1️⃣ Struggling — demos beautifully, breaks under real load, data and users.
2️⃣ Maturing — reliable in production… but the discipline is reinvented team by team.
3️⃣ Enterprise — production-readiness becomes an organisational property: shared standards, central scaffolding, enforced gates, portfolio audit.

Here's the trap: Stage 2 feels like winning. It isn't. Thirty well-built projects are still thirty bespoke audits and thirty different ways to break — and the risk lands on a sponsor who never saw the decisions.

The unlock isn't more skilled teams. It's a central AI factory — one place that makes production-readiness the default instead of per-team heroics.

That's where I'm focused.
