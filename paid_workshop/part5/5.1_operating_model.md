# Part 5.1 — The Operating Model: Who Runs the AI Factory

**Maven one-liner:** The full org structure that houses the four enforcement duties from Part 3 — CoE, architect, engineering, product, governance, pods, and the independent ARB/CRO line.

## The point of this subpart

Part 3.4 named the four enforcement duties — define / build / operate / judge. This subpart shows the **org that those duties live inside**: who funds it, who runs it, who certifies it, and how the independence line is preserved. It's the "who actually does this in a real company" picture.

## Above the function

- **AI Steering Committee** — sets the agenda and funds the quarter's builds.
- **Chief Enterprise Architect** — the AI catalogue is the AI domain of EA; this is where it connects to the rest of Enterprise Architecture.

## Inside the AI CoE

- **Head of AI CoE** — takes the budget, builds the capability.
- **Lead AI Architect — the WHAT.** Owns the catalogue (the standards and what "pass" means). The architecture discipline sits here; architects deploy into pods on a dotted line.
- **Head of Engineering — the BUILD.** Ships the standards. Runs the delivery **pods** (PM lead, architect dotted-in, 2–3 developers, platform engineer), ~2 pillars per pod, re-pointed each quarter.
- **Head of Product — the RUN.** Owns SLA and lifecycle: Run/Platform Eng (on-call), Adoption/Enablement (embeds with project teams), Migration/Retrofit (moves teams onto the SDK).
- **Head of Governance — CERTIFY.** Owns change control and the attestation/ARB gate definitions. Governance specialists embed into Architecture on a dotted line but report out solid — that's the independence.

## Outside the function — enterprise authority

- **AI Architecture Review** — deep AI certification; prepares evidence.
- **Enterprise ARB** — final, enterprise-wide authority; spot-check and portfolio view.
- **Enterprise Risk / Compliance (CRO)** — owns governance authority and feeds it to the ARB.

The deliberate design: the people who **build and run** standards sit inside the CoE; the people who **judge** them report outside it. Same rule as Part 3.4 — independence is structural, not goodwill.

## Takeaway for this subpart

> The four duties from Part 3 aren't roles on a slide — they're a real org: a CoE that builds and runs the standards, funded by a steering committee, with governance and the ARB sitting outside the function so the verdict stays independent.
