# Part 5.3 — Aegis: The Live Standards Repository

**Maven one-liner:** Aegis is the working catalogue of enterprise AI standards, reachable from your own Cowork account via the MCP connector — and cohort members get one month free access plus the 25 standards we've already built.

## The point of this subpart

Across Parts 2–4 the cohort built standards *by hand*. Aegis is the same thing, **real and at scale** — a live, queryable repository of enterprise AI architecture standards. This subpart closes the workshop by showing the tool, connecting it to each student's environment, and handing over access.

---

## A — What Aegis is (the teaching)

Aegis is a **repository of enterprise AI architecture standards, fully accessible via a Claude MCP connector.** Each standard carries the full anatomy the cohort just built in Part 4 — statement, problem, solution, gates, framework mappings, ARB evidence, and a concrete reference implementation.

- It's the manual Part 4 exercise, productised: instead of filling twelve fields by hand for every standard, you query a catalogue that already has them.
- **Scope today:** currently GenAI-focused, rapidly evolving to include ML and the wider AWS Well-Architected standards.
- **How it's reached:** through the MCP connector, so the catalogue is available directly inside Cowork / Claude — searchable, filterable by pillar, focus area, maturity, and framework mapping.

This is the natural endpoint of the workshop's arc: Part 1 showed why standards matter, Parts 2–4 showed how to build one, and Aegis is where a maintained catalogue of them lives.

---

## B — What cohort members get (the offer)

Kept separate from the teaching on purpose — this is the access students receive:

- **The MCP connector** — connect Aegis to their **own Cowork account**, so they can query the catalogue from their own environment.
- **One month free access** to the Aegis repository.
- **The 25 standards** we've already built — ready to query, adapt, and enforce in their own work.

So they don't just leave with the *method*; they leave with a *working catalogue* of 25 standards and the connector to use it from day one.

## Handoff from Part 4

Part 4 ended with one standard built slowly, by hand. Aegis is the answer to "now do that for the whole estate without doing it manually" — the tool that turns the method into a maintained, enforceable catalogue.

## Takeaway for this subpart — and the close of the workshop

> You learned to build a standard by hand; Aegis is where a maintained catalogue of them lives. Connect it to your own Cowork account, and you walk away with 25 ready-to-use standards and the connector to query them — the method *and* the tool.
