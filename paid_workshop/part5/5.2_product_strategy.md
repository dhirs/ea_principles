# Part 5.2 — Product Strategy: The Adoption Journey

**Maven one-liner:** How enforcement rolls out across the estate over time — self-attestation → EA spot-checks → central SDK — the same three rungs as Part 3, viewed as an org-wide journey, not a per-control choice.

## The point of this subpart

Part 3.3 graded enforcement strength **per control** (attest / verify / provide) — a capability portfolio where different controls sit at different rungs. This subpart reframes the same three rungs as a **product strategy over time**: how the whole enforcement capability matures across the organisation, phase by phase. Same ladder, different axis — *time and org-wide rollout* instead of *per-control choice*.

## The three phases

1. **Phase 1 — Self-attestation.** Projects certify themselves against the standard. Cheap, immediate, no tooling. (The org-wide version of L1 Attest.)
2. **Phase 2 — EA spot-checks.** Central EA audits projects at random — trust, but verify. (The org-wide version of L2 Verify.)
3. **Phase 3 — Central SDK.** Enforcement is automated and baked into a central SDK + enforced routing. (The org-wide version of L3 Provide.)

## The lesson to land

The path runs attestation → automation, and **each rung is stronger, costlier, and harder to commit to.** The warning from Part 3 carries straight over here: *most enterprises stop at Phase 1* — commitment ends at the cheapest rung, and that is exactly why AI fails beyond the demo. The product strategy is the deliberate plan to **not** stall there: enable before you enforce, fund the climb, and move the estate up the rungs on purpose.

## How to frame it against 3.3 (avoid repeating)

- **3.3 answered:** for *this control*, what enforcement strength is appropriate right now? (per-control)
- **5.2 answers:** for *the organisation*, how do we mature the whole enforcement capability over time? (org-wide, phased)

Say it explicitly in the room — "you've seen the rungs per control; here's how the org climbs them as a product." That turns overlap into reinforcement.

## Takeaway for this subpart

> Enforcement isn't just a per-control setting — it's a product with a roadmap. Self-attestation → spot-checks → central SDK is the adoption journey, and the whole strategy exists to stop the org from parking permanently at the cheapest rung.
