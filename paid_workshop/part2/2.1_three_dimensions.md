# Part 2.1 — Three Dimensions, But Not Three Frameworks

**Maven one-liner:** Why Legal & Risk is the only dimension with external anchors — and Cost and CX have to be built bespoke.

## The point of this subpart

Every standard in the catalogue serves exactly one of three dimensions:

- **Legal & Risk** — what the business is *liable* for.
- **Customer Experience** — *trust* in the product.
- **Cost** — *spend vs. value*.

The naive expectation is that each dimension has a framework you can baseline against. It doesn't. **Only one of the three comes with an external source of truth.** That asymmetry is the whole reason catalogue-building has a defined starting point — and it's the idea this subpart exists to plant.

## Why Cost and CX are contextual

There is no global, authoritative framework that tells you what "good cost discipline" or "good customer experience" is for *your* AI workloads, because both are a function of things only you know:

- **Cost** depends on your traffic, your margins, your model mix, your latency tolerance, what you're willing to spend per call. A token-burn threshold that's reckless for one workload is trivial for another.
- **CX** depends on your brand voice, your risk appetite for hallucination, what "good enough" accuracy means for the task, your tone and escalation rules.

So for these two, you don't inherit a baseline — you **set your own bar from first principles** and back it with engineering heuristics (the AWS GenAI Lens helps on the *technical* shape, but it can't tell you where *your* line sits). These standards are authored, not adopted.

## Why Legal & Risk is different

Legal & Risk is the one dimension where **you don't get to invent the baseline.** It is inherited from whatever instrument creates legal liability — the EU AI Act, GDPR, FCA rules, sector regulation, your internal risk register. The outside world hands you the "must."

That makes it the natural place to *start* building the catalogue: it's the dimension with concrete, external, non-negotiable inputs, and the dimension where getting it wrong is most expensive. Cost and CX you can iterate on; liability you cannot.

## Takeaway for this subpart

> Three dimensions, but only one framework-anchored. Cost and CX are contextual — you set the bar yourself. Legal & Risk is inherited — so that's where catalogue-building begins.
