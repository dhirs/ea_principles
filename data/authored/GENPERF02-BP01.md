- 2026-06-07 | GENPERF02-BP01 (whole BP — "Load test model endpoints"; AWS verbatim fetched 2026-06-07, 3 steps, risk **Medium**) | not_promoted. GENPERF02 ("How do you verify your generative AI workload maintains acceptable performance levels?", focus area "Maintaining model performance") ships three BPs; all three not_promoted (see the two entries below). BP01 mandate: load-test the model endpoint to establish a performance baseline under average + peak throughput, then use the baseline to inform monitoring/alert thresholds and endpoint selection (3 steps: reference the org AI policy for perf metrics; build a load-testing harness that prompts at configurable rates against golden datasets; collect results and locate the bottleneck). **User directed not_promote 2026-06-07** after an interactive HALT in which the carve-out case was explicitly weighed. Decisive ground: **not GenAI-distinct.** Load testing to establish a capacity baseline and set alert thresholds is generic performance engineering already owned by the base Well-Architected framework — the BP cross-references `PERF05-BP04` and four `MLPER` ML-lens items — with only a thin GenAI veneer ("use a golden prompt dataset"; "remediate via prompt caching / batch inference"). Promoting a generic infra discipline into the GenAI catalogue adds little and would not survive an ARB reader asking "why is load-testing a GenAI principle?" Supporting grounds: (a) **decomposition smell / overlap** — the promote-able core splits cleanly, the SLO + alert-threshold half into observability (GO3B2 family), the golden-dataset half into the GO1B1 eval harness and the still-UNMAPPED GENPERF01-BP01 (define ground truth for performance); what is uniquely left ("run a load test before deploying an endpoint you own") is thin, pre_deploy, and process-shaped — a test you run, not a committable artefact a lint can bite on, so the gate would be low-signal ("a load_test_result.yaml exists and is fresh") and gameable next to the GO3B1-01 AST lint / GC4B1-01 deployed-vs-declared parser. (b) **Serving-paradigm narrowness** — only bites for self_hosted_* / api_provisioned endpoints you own; pure managed-API consumers (Bedrock on-demand / OpenAI / Anthropic) can't load-test an endpoint the provider owns and scales (this was discounted on its own — GC2B2-01 is also self-hosting-only and promoted — but compounds the generic-discipline problem). step_promotion would fail architecturally_distinct (~1, generic infra discipline + redundant with observability/eval) and has_enforceable_artefact (~1-2, process-shaped pre_deploy test). Noted flip condition: an audience heavily self-hosting (regulated enterprises running their own vLLM/SageMaker clusters) could justify a GenAI-flavoured load-test principle as a deliberate worked example; not the default, and the user did not take it.
