# Head of Governance — Job Spec

**Mission:** make AI at the enterprise safe, compliant, and defensible — and prove it.
**Reports to:** enterprise Risk / Compliance (CRO) — **solid line, outside the AI function** (independence). Dotted line into the Head of AI CoE.
**Owns the people:** AI governance / risk specialists, embedded (matrixed) into the architecture and delivery teams.

## Full remit
1. **Responsible AI policy & framework** — fairness, explainability, safety, human-oversight rules every AI system follows.
2. **Regulatory compliance** — EU AI Act, FCA, GDPR, sector rules; classification of AI systems by risk tier.
3. **Model risk management** — model validation, bias/safety testing, the AI risk register, incident governance.
4. **Certification & change-control** — the governance wrapper on each standard: audit sheet, ARB gate, change & waiver process. *(One workstream — see below.)*
5. **Evidence & audit** — the audit trail that satisfies the ARB and regulators.

## Day-to-day
- Maintain the responsible-AI policy and risk-tiering; classify incoming use cases by risk.
- Run model-risk reviews — validation, bias/safety testing on AI systems before and after release.
- Adjudicate waivers; own the AI risk register and AI incident governance.
- Direct embedded specialists who co-author standards' governance wrappers with architects.
- Prepare cert evidence; feed the AI Architecture Review and Enterprise ARB; own regulatory mapping.

## Certification & change-control (one section of the role)
Owns the process and templates that wrap each standard — audit/attestation sheet, ARB gate definition, change-control and waivers. Owns the *process*; the architect owns content; Platform owns the schema.

## Owns vs doesn't own
- **Owns:** responsible-AI policy, regulatory compliance, model risk, certification process, audit trail, risk register.
- **Does NOT own:** standard/architecture content (architect), the config schema (Platform/Product), final sign-off (Enterprise ARB — independent).

## Key seams — independence is the point
- Solid line goes **out** to the CRO, not to the Head of AI CoE — governance's pay can't depend on the CoE shipping.
- The AI function authors and prepares; the **Enterprise ARB approves**. These never merge.
- Specialists embed (dotted) in Architecture so wrappers match standards — but answer to *this* line.

## Cadence
Risk reviews per use case · waiver reviews weekly · model-risk validation per release · regulatory-mapping & policy review quarterly · Enterprise ARB at milestones.
