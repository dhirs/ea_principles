Four pillars + independent governance

The CoE with Governance added as a fourth function — dotted into the CoE, solid out to enterprise Risk — and the ARB sitting outside the AI function.

reporting line dotted — governance / deployment independent authority governance / handoff gate

The rule the whole structure protects: the AI function authors and prepares; the enterprise ARB approves. They never collapse into one body. The moment the people who write the standards also sign them off, the independence that made the governance story worth telling is gone.

Who does what — the governance additions

4th function · dotted-in / solid-out

Head of Governance

  • Peer to the Lead Architect; dotted line into the Head of AI CoE.
  • Solid line out to enterprise Risk / Compliance (CRO) — that line is what keeps governance independent of delivery.
  • Supplies governance specialists who embed in the architecture team.
Accountable for: certification and change-control being credible.
Embedded in Architecture

Governance specialists

  • Co-author each standard's governance wrapper with the architect — the audit sheet, ARB gate, change & waiver process.
  • Dotted line to the Head of Governance; work day-to-day under the Lead Architect.
  • Own the process and templates; the architect owns the standard's content; Platform owns the config schema.
Accountable for: the wrapper matching the standard, and changing cleanly.
Outside AI · deep tier

AI Architecture Review

  • Does the detailed, AI-specific certification a generalist board can't.
  • Prepares the evidence and feeds the Enterprise ARB.
  • Stops the enterprise ARB from either bottlenecking or rubber-stamping AI.
Accountable for: depth of the AI cert.
Outside AI · final authority

Enterprise ARB

  • Enterprise-wide — governs all architecture, not just AI; AI is one domain it enforces.
  • Spot-check and portfolio-level review; holds final sign-off.
  • Draws its authority from enterprise Risk / Compliance — outside the AI function entirely.
Accountable for: the gate having teeth a regulator respects.

Carried over from v1 (unchanged):

• Architects belong to the architecture line, deployed (dotted) into pods — the catalogue stays one product.

• Build → run readiness gate between Engineering and Product — Product can't inherit what it can't operate.

• Grow-into: Head of PM at ~5+ pods; split Platform out of delivery at ~15–20 live standards.
