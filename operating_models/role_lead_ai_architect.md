# Lead AI Architect — Job Spec

**Mission:** the design authority for AI across the enterprise — make AI systems coherent, buildable, and production-ready by design.
**Reports to:** Head of AI CoE (solid). Dotted line to the Chief Enterprise Architect (AI as a domain of enterprise EA).
**Owns the people:** the architecture discipline — all AI architects (solid line), deployed into pods and use-case teams (dotted).

## Full remit
1. **Reference architectures** — the blessed patterns for AI systems (RAG, agentic, fine-tuning, model-gateway) that every use case builds from.
2. **Technology & model selection** — the AI tech radar; recommend the tools, models, and vendors (Langfuse vs LangSmith, model providers). Author the ADRs; ratify through the ARB.
3. **Design authority** — review major AI solution designs before build; the technical gate on big use cases.
4. **The standards catalogue** — principles, standards, sized blueprints. *(One workstream — see below.)*
5. **Architecture consistency** — keep the AI estate from fragmenting across BUs and pods.

## Day-to-day
- Review incoming use-case designs against the reference architectures; unblock teams on design.
- Run the tech-radar: evaluate tools/models, write ADRs, take recommendations to the ARB.
- Direct the architecture discipline; keep conventions uniform across pods and use cases.
- Author/curate the standards catalogue and co-design each standard's governance wrapper with the embedded governance specialist.
- Curate the build backlog; defend priorities into the quarterly prioritisation.

## Standards & catalogue (one section of the role)
Owns technical correctness and cross-catalogue consistency of the principles/standards. Holds the pen on standard content; pods build the reference implementations under architects deployed from this discipline.

## Owns vs doesn't own
- **Owns:** AI architecture, reference patterns, tech/model selection (recommendation), design authority, the standards catalogue.
- **Does NOT own:** which use cases get funded (BUs / Steering Committee), delivery schedule (Head of Eng), the SLA (Head of Product), governance sign-off (ARB).

## Key seams
- Architects belong to *this* line, deployed (dotted) into pods/use-cases — the estate stays coherent.
- Tech selection is recommend-and-ratify, not unilateral: architect proposes, security vets, ARB makes it the standard.
- Roadmap co-owned with Head of Product: architect owns correctness, Product owns value/lifecycle.

## Cadence
Design reviews continuous · tech-radar review quarterly · backlog grooming weekly · ARB per major decision.
