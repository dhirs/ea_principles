# Head of Engineering — Job Spec

**Mission:** build and run the AI engineering capability — the platform every AI system stands on, and the delivery muscle that ships use cases.
**Reports to:** Head of AI CoE (solid).
**Owns the people:** the AI engineering org — platform engineers, developers, the delivery pods and their POD Lead PMs. (Pod architects are dotted-in from the Architecture line.)

## Full remit
1. **The AI platform** — model gateway, MLOps/LLMOps, the central SDK (guardrails, eval harness), integration to feature/vector stores. Build it and keep it engineered.
2. **Use-case delivery** — the build capacity that turns funded BU use cases into shipped AI systems on the platform.
3. **Standards reference implementations** — the pods that build standards into enforceable RIs. *(One workstream — see below.)*
4. **Engineering practice** — quality, CI/CD, tooling, test discipline across all AI builds.

## Day-to-day
- Run the engineering org: staff platform work and use-case builds against the funded agenda.
- Own platform roadmap delivery — gateway, SDK, MLOps capabilities the whole estate depends on.
- Stand up delivery pods for prioritised use cases and standards; re-point them each quarter.
- Run the build → run **readiness gate**: nothing ships to Product until it's operable.
- Unblock delivery — dependencies, environments, hiring/contractor flow; surface slippage early.

## Standards & RIs (one section of the role)
Runs the pods that build standards into reference implementations — project-shaped work, build/ship/disband. Permanent ownership of what they produce passes to Product (run) and Architecture (content).

## Owns vs doesn't own
- **Owns:** platform build, use-case delivery, engineering quality, the schedule, the handoff to Product.
- **Does NOT own:** what a standard/architecture says (architect), use-case funding (BUs / Steering Committee), the live SLA (Product), certification (Governance).

## Key seams
- Build vs run: Engineering builds; Product owns the SLA. The readiness gate protects Product from inheriting what it can't operate.
- PM owns *delivery*, architect owns *content* — don't let the PM chain take over design.
- External agencies build *on* the platform to the standards — not bypassing it.

## Cadence
Pod stand-ups daily · platform & delivery review weekly · readiness gate per build · pod re-pointing quarterly.
